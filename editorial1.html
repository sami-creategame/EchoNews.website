The Paradox of Connectivity

Home Editorials
Editorial

Written by MERCIER Alexis, On January 3, 2026

In the article, I mentioned the alarming statistics regarding our smartphone usage and rising loneliness. However, numbers only tell half the story. The reality is that we are living a paradox: we have never been so reachable, yet we have rarely been so out of touch.

We often confuse "connectivity" with "connection." Social media gives us the illusion of being surrounded by friends. We see their photos and read their updates, so we feel involved. But this is a passive experience. Sending a "like" or an emoji is not the same as hearing a friend’s voice or seeing them smile. We are snacking on digital interactions, but we remain hungry for true human contact.

The real danger is that our devices steal our attention from the present moment. When we check a notification during a dinner or a conversation, we send a hurtful message to the people around us: "This screen is more important than you." By trying to be everywhere at once online, we end up being nowhere in real life. We risk losing the social skills that make us human, such as empathy and deep listening.

Technology is a brilliant tool, but a terrible master. We should not throw away our phones, but we must stop using them as a shield against the real world. The solution is simple but requires courage: we need to disconnect to reconnect. Let’s use our devices to arrange a meeting, and then put them away to actually enjoy it. As you read the rest of our news site, ask yourself: when was the last time you had a conversation without looking at your screen?
